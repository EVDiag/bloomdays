Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 4, 2026 · Bloom · v1.0

Welcome to Bloom. This privacy policy explains, in plain language, what data Bloom collects, how it's used, and the choices you have. We've tried to make this readable rather than legally dense — but it's still a binding agreement, so please read carefully.

The short version:

  • Almost all your data stays on your device. We can't read it.
  • We never sell your data to advertisers, brokers, or anyone else.
  • You can export or delete everything, anytime.
  • We collect minimal anonymous analytics to improve the app — nothing that identifies you.

1. Who we are

Bloom (the "App") is operated by [YOUR LEGAL NAME OR COMPANY] ("we", "us", "our"), based in [YOUR COUNTRY/STATE]. You can reach us at privacy@bloom.app.

2. What data we collect

2.1 Data you provide directly

When you use Bloom, you may enter information such as:

  • Your name (or chosen nickname) and partner's name
  • Birthday and due date
  • Pregnancy mode (cycle, trying, pregnant, newborn)
  • Daily logs: symptoms, mood, weight, food intake, water intake, exercise
  • Reproductive health data: period dates, ovulation, basal temperature, intimacy log
  • Pregnancy data: kicks, contractions, weight changes, photos
  • Newborn data: feedings, diapers, sleep, growth measurements
  • Journal entries and notes
  • Appointments and reminders

2.2 Data automatically collected

When you use the app, we automatically collect:

  • Anonymous app usage events (which screens you visit, errors you encounter)
  • Device model, operating system version, app version, language and country
  • A randomly-generated installation ID (not linked to your identity)

We do not collect your IP address for tracking, your contacts, your location, your phone number, or your email (unless you explicitly provide it).

2.3 Data from purchases

When you purchase Bloom Pro or AI credits, the transaction is handled by Google Play Billing. We receive a confirmation that you purchased the item, but Google handles your payment information. We never see your card details.

3. How your data is stored

Bloom is local-first. Your personal data — symptoms, logs, journal entries, photos — is stored on your device. We don't see it. We don't transmit it to our servers unless you explicitly enable a feature that requires it (e.g. partner sync, when launched).

If we add cloud sync in a future version, it will be:

  • Opt-in only (off by default)
  • End-to-end encrypted, meaning we cannot read your synced data even if we wanted to
  • Deletable on demand from within the app

4. How we use your data

The data on your device is used to power the app's features — show you what week you're in, suggest meals, predict your cycle, and so on. None of this leaves your device.

The anonymous analytics we collect are used to:

  • Identify and fix crashes and bugs
  • Understand which features are used (so we can prioritize improvements)
  • Measure overall app performance

We do not use your data to:

  • Show you ads (Bloom is ad-free)
  • Sell to advertisers, data brokers, insurance companies, or any third party
  • Train AI models on your personal information
  • Make inferences about your identity or share anything with employers

5. Who we share data with

We share data only with these limited categories of service providers, only as needed to operate the app:

  • Google Play Billing — to process purchases. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Firebase Analytics & Crashlytics — for anonymous usage analytics and crash reporting. Subject to Firebase Privacy.
  • Anthropic — when (and only when) you use the AI assistant feature, your specific question is sent to Anthropic's API to generate a response. Anthropic does not retain or train on this data per their terms. Your question content does not include any of your personal logs unless you explicitly include them in the question.

We will never share your data with advertisers, data brokers, employers, insurers, or government agencies (except as legally required by court order — which we will challenge where lawful).

6. Your rights and controls

You have the right to:

  • Access all data we hold about you. Most of this is visible in the app itself. Email privacy@bloom.app for a full export.
  • Correct inaccurate data. You can edit or delete entries anytime in the app.
  • Delete your data. Settings → Privacy → Delete all data wipes everything from your device. To delete server-side data (analytics), email us.
  • Port your data. Pro users can export full PDF reports and CSV files of their logs.
  • Withdraw consent for analytics in Settings → Privacy → Anonymous analytics.

For users in the EU/UK (GDPR): The legal basis for processing is your consent (which you give by using the app and accepting this policy) and our legitimate interest in providing and improving the service. You can also lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority.

For users in California (CCPA/CPRA): You have the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete it, and the right to opt out of "sale" — but we don't sell your data, ever, so the third doesn't apply.

7. Children's privacy

Bloom is not intended for users under 13 (or under 16 in the EU). If we discover we've collected data from a child below this age, we will delete it.

8. Health information disclaimer

Bloom provides general information based on medical literature, not personal medical advice. We are not a doctor. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis, treatment, or any health concern.

Pregnancy and fertility information is sensitive. We've designed Bloom with care for users in jurisdictions where reproductive privacy may be at risk. Local-first storage means your reproductive data is much harder to access by third parties — including via subpoena — than it would be on a cloud-first app.

9. Security

We use industry-standard security practices, including:

  • Local data encrypted at rest using your device's secure storage
  • HTTPS-only connections for all network traffic
  • Minimal data collection (we can't leak what we don't have)
  • Regular security audits

However, no system is 100% secure. If a breach occurs, we'll notify affected users within 72 hours and report to relevant authorities as required by law.

10. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy in a way that meaningfully changes how your data is handled, we'll notify you in the app. Continued use after changes means you accept the new policy.
